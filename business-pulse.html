📈 Business Pulse Intelligence Brief

SEPTEMBER 2025 EDITION

Key Finding: The latest BTOS data shows business performance holding near summer highs at 56.5 (down 0.3), well above the low-50s levels that dominated most of 2025. Revenue conditions remain at 42.1, consistent with the persistently challenging 40-43 range seen throughout the year. Cost pressures continue at severely elevated levels with input prices at 69.3 (down 1.0 from 70.3), representing typical volatility within the broader upward cost trend that has characterized 2025. Output prices also decreased slightly to 55.7, but remain within the elevated range. Full-year analysis reveals the dramatic summer performance surge as the key 2025 story, alongside persistent cost pressures and revenue challenges.

📈 Summer Performance Surge Sustained

Major 2025 Development: Business performance experienced a dramatic surge in mid-July (period 15) from the low-50s range that dominated most of 2025 to nearly 57, and has sustained these elevated levels through the current period at 56.5. This represents the most significant positive development of 2025. Cost pressures remain within the elevated 62-70 range that has characterized the entire year, with the recent 1.0-point decline representing typical week-to-week volatility within the broader upward trend. The persistent gap between input costs (69.3) and output prices (55.7) continues to create substantial margin compression challenges throughout 2025.

📊 About The Business Pulse Analysis

BTOS Index Methodology: Values above 50 indicate net positive sentiment (more increases than decreases), while values below 50 indicate net negative sentiment. The indices are calculated from business owner responses about changes in their operations over the past two weeks compared to the prior two-week period. Our analysis synthesizes ~1.2M business responses into actionable economic intelligence covering all 18 bi-weekly periods in 2025.

Pricing Dynamics & Cost Pressures

💰 What This Measures

Survey Questions: "How did the prices this business pays for goods or services change in the last two weeks?" (input costs) and "How did the prices this business charges for its own goods or services change in the last two weeks?" (output prices). These track inflation pressures from both cost and revenue perspectives.

📊 Sustained High-Cost Environment

Cost pressures remain severely elevated with the input price index at 69.3, continuing the high-cost environment that has persisted throughout 2025. Input prices have fluctuated within a 62-70 range all year, with 40.0% of businesses reporting increased input costs versus only 1.4% reporting decreases in the current period. The recent 1.0-point decline from 70.3 represents normal week-to-week volatility within the overall upward trend. The 13.6-point gap between input costs (69.3) and output prices (55.7) reflects the ongoing margin compression challenges that have characterized the entire year, as businesses struggle to pass through the sustained cost increases to consumers.

6-Month Economic Outlook

🔮 What This Measures

Survey Questions: Business owners are asked about their expectations six months from now (around February 2026) across multiple areas: performance, employment levels, hours worked, delivery times, inventory levels, demand, and pricing. These forward-looking indicators help predict economic trends.

💡 Forward-Looking Economic Indicators

Business expectations for the next six months (through February 2026) show cautious expectations despite current elevated performance levels. Businesses expect modest deterioration in performance (54.9 vs current 56.5), suggesting they view the summer surge as temporary. Employment expectations (51.0) remain slightly above neutral but show minimal optimism. Most concerning is that businesses anticipate severe future cost pressures to persist, with input prices expected to reach 76.8—far above current levels of 69.3. Future demand expectations (51.7) indicate modest recovery hopes, but overall sentiment suggests businesses view current challenges as persistent rather than temporary.

📈 Key Outlook Insights

Cautious expectations despite current elevated performance: While businesses anticipate modest demand recovery (+8.5 points to 51.7) and employment improvements (+3.2 points to 51.0), they expect performance to moderate (-1.6 points to 54.9) from current summer highs back toward the low-50s range that dominated most of 2025. Most concerning is the expectation of even more severe cost pressures, with future input prices expected to reach 76.8 (+7.5 points), well above the already elevated 62-70 range that characterized 2025, suggesting businesses view cost inflation as intensifying rather than moderating.
