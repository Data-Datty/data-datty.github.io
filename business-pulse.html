📈 Business Pulse Intelligence Brief

AUGUST 2025 EDITION

Key Finding: The latest BTOS data shows sustained strong business performance sentiment with the performance index holding steady at 56.9 (unchanged). Revenue conditions improved modestly with the index rising to 42.7 from 41.8. Cost pressures remain severe with input prices at 68.7 (down slightly from 69.9) and output prices at 55.5 (down from 56.2). Notably, businesses rate current performance higher than future expectations, suggesting growing pessimism about near-term prospects.

📉 Performance Expectation Gap

Notable Finding: Businesses rate current performance at 56.9 but expect it to decline to 55.3 in six months. This pessimistic outlook, combined with expectations of rising future input costs (75.9), suggests businesses anticipate more challenging conditions ahead despite current relative stability.

📊 About The Business Pulse Analysis

BTOS Index Methodology: Values above 50 indicate net positive sentiment (more increases than decreases), while values below 50 indicate net negative sentiment. The indices are calculated from business owner responses about changes in their operations over the past two weeks compared to the prior two-week period. Our analysis synthesizes ~1.2M business responses into actionable economic intelligence.

Pricing Dynamics & Cost Pressures

💰 What This Measures

Survey Questions: "How did the prices this business pays for goods or services change in the last two weeks?" (input costs) and "How did the prices this business charges for its own goods or services change in the last two weeks?" (output prices). 💰 Persistent Cost Pressures

Input cost pressures remain severe with the index at 68.7 (down modestly from 69.9). While this represents a slight improvement, costs are still far above the neutral 50 level. Output prices also declined slightly to 55.5 from 56.2. The gap between input and output price pressures remains substantial, indicating continued margin challenges for businesses.

6-Month Economic Outlook

🔮 What This Measures

Survey Questions: Business owners are asked about their expectations six months from now (around February 2026) across multiple areas: performance, employment levels, hours worked, delivery times, inventory levels, demand, and pricing. These forward-looking indicators help predict economic trends.

💡 Forward-Looking Economic Indicators

Business expectations for the next six months (through February 2026) reveal growing pessimism about the economic outlook. Notably, businesses rate current performance at 56.9 but expect it to decline to 55.3 by early 2026—a rare instance where current conditions are viewed more favorably than future prospects. Employment expectations (50.8) show modest optimism, but businesses anticipate significantly higher cost pressures with future input prices at 75.9, suggesting they view current cost relief as temporary.

📈 Key Outlook Insights

Growing business pessimism about near-term outlook: The most striking finding is that businesses rate current performance (56.9) higher than expected future performance (55.3)—an unusual pattern that suggests underlying concerns about economic trajectory. While businesses expect modest demand recovery (+7.5 points) and employment growth (+2.7 points), they anticipate severe cost pressure resurgence (+7.2 points to 75.9), indicating they view the current slight easing in input costs as temporary relief rather than a sustainable trend.
