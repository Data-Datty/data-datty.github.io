📈 Business Pulse Intelligence Brief

JULY 2025 EDITION

Key Finding: The latest BTOS data shows significant improvement in business performance sentiment with the performance index rising to 56.9 from 52.3. However, revenue pressures persist with 32.0% reporting decreased revenue vs 15.6% increased. Input cost pressures have intensified to 69.9 index vs 56.2 output price index, creating margin challenges heading into late 2025. Employment conditions show modest net growth with 12.8% increasing vs 10.5% decreasing employees.

📊 About The Business Pulse Analysis

BTOS Index Methodology: Values above 50 indicate net positive sentiment (more increases than decreases), while values below 50 indicate net negative sentiment. The indices are calculated from business owner responses about changes in their operations over the past two weeks compared to the prior two-week period. Our analysis synthesizes ~1.2M business responses into actionable economic intelligence.

6-Month Economic Outlook

🔮 What This Measures

Survey Questions: Business owners are asked about their expectations six months from now (around January 2026) across multiple areas: performance ("How do you think you will describe this business's performance?"), employment levels, hours worked, delivery times, inventory levels, demand, and pricing. These forward-looking indicators help predict economic trends.

💡 Forward-Looking Economic Indicators

Business expectations for the next six months (through January 2026) show cautious optimism tempered by cost concerns. The future performance index (55.6) remains strong, while employment expectations (51.2) indicate continued modest hiring plans. However, businesses expect cost pressures to moderate slightly with future input prices at 72.9 compared to current 69.9, though still elevated.

📈 Key Outlook Insights

Mixed expectations for early 2026: While businesses expect significant demand recovery (+8.4 points) and continued employment growth (+3.0 points) by January 2026, they also anticipate further intensification of cost pressures (+3.0 points on input prices to 72.9).
